Why Omega-3 matters so much
during perimenopause

During perimenopause, oestrogen and progesterone levels fluctuate dramatically. Omega-3 fatty acids play a crucial role — across multiple systems at once.

71%
Of women are deficient in Omega-3
Most women don't eat enough oily fish or take supplements. During perimenopause, the need is even higher than usual.
3–6 months
Before you feel the full benefit
Omega-3 isn't a quick fix. Consistent use over several months delivers the best results — better sleep, fewer hot flashes, improved focus.
EPA & DHA
The two fatty acids that matter most
EPA reduces inflammation and supports mood. DHA is essential for brain function — that "brain fog" so many women experience is partly a DHA deficiency.

"Oestrogen regulates how your body processes omega-3. As oestrogen declines, you actually need more omega-3 — not less."

5 benefits of Omega-3
during perimenopause

These are the areas where women in perimenopause notice the biggest difference when they get enough Omega-3.

🧠
Less brain fog
DHA is a structural component of brain cells. Adequate DHA improves concentration, memory and mental clarity.
😴
Better sleep
Omega-3 boosts melatonin production and reduces night sweats — two complaints that often go hand in hand during perimenopause.
🌡
Fewer hot flashes
EPA's anti-inflammatory action can reduce both the frequency and intensity of hot flashes.
💪
Better joint health
Joint pain increases as oestrogen drops. Omega-3 lubricates joints and inhibits inflammatory processes.
😊
More stable mood
EPA helps regulate serotonin and dopamine — the neurotransmitters that determine your mood.
❤️
Heart & vascular health
Cardiovascular risk rises after menopause. Omega-3 protects the heart and lowers triglycerides.

The best sources of Omega-3

Food first — but for most women, supplementation is necessary to reach adequate levels.

1
Oily fish (2–3x per week)
Salmon, mackerel, herring, sardines and anchovies are the richest sources. Note: farmed fish contains significantly less EPA/DHA than wild-caught.
2
Flaxseed & chia seeds
These contain ALA (a plant-based omega-3), but your body converts only ~5% into EPA/DHA. Not sufficient as a sole source.
3
Omega-3 supplements (fish oil)
Choose supplements with at least 1000 mg EPA+DHA per day. Check for purity (no heavy metals) and freshness.
4
Algae oil (vegan option)
Fish get their omega-3 from algae. Algae oil skips the middleman and is equally effective — and more sustainable.

Quality makes the difference. Many fish oils on the market are already oxidised before you open the bottle. Choose fresh, molecularly distilled omega-3 with a high concentration of EPA and DHA. Cost per dose matters more than cost per capsule.
